Privacy Policy & Digital Ethics

Welcome to the PS2 BIOS Hub. We are deeply committed to protecting the privacy of the preservation community. This document outlines our data handling practices, our compliance with global privacy standards, and our ethical stance on the digital rights of our users.

1. Zero-Collection Data Philosophy

Unlike many modern web portals that rely on aggressive user tracking and behavioral analysis, the PS2 BIOS project operates on a "Zero-Collection" philosophy. We do not utilize cookies, we do not implement third-party tracking pixels (such as Google Analytics or Meta Pixel), and we do not store IP addresses in our application logs beyond their standard server-side TTL (Time to Live) for DDOS protection.

When you browse our guides or download MD5-verified BIOS packs, your activity remains anonymous. We believe that privacy is a fundamental pillar of hardware preservation research.

2. GDPR and International Compliance

Although we do not collect personal data, we adhere to the spirit of the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and the **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)**. Our users have a right to access, rectify, and delete their data. Since we do not collect such data, these requests essentially result in a confirmation of user anonymity.

Pro-Privacy Tip

For maximum anonymity when accessing preservation resources, we recommend using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) and a browser with Canvas-fingerprinting protection enabled.

3. Log File Handling and Security

As part of our commitment to transparency, we must state that standard web server logs are maintained for a period of 14 days solely for security auditing. This data is strictly used to identify malicious bot activity and brute-force attacks on our infrastructure. These logs contain:

  • Browser user-agent strings (for accessibility auditing)
  • Referrer URLs (to understand how users find our archive)
  • Timestamp of access
None of this data is linked to personal identity or used for advertising purposes.

4. Third-Party Links and Emulation Resources

Our site contains links to external preservation projects (such as GitHub, PCSX2, and EmuGen). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage users to read the individual privacy policies of any platform they visit after leaving the PS2 BIOS Hub.

5. Fair Use and Educational Intent

The content provided on this platform is for educational, historical, and preservation purposes only. We operate under the "Transformative Use" and "Interoperability" exceptions of global copyright law. By using this site, you acknowledge that you are responsible for maintaining compliance with the intellectual property laws of your specific jurisdiction.

6. Updates to this Policy

As digital privacy landscapes evolve, so too will this policy. The last major revision was completed on April 8, 2026. Any future changes will be posted directly to this URL with a clear changelog summary.
