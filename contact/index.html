Technical Support & Outreach

We welcome technical inquiries regarding BIOS compatibility, hardware dumping procedures, and preservation ethics. Before reaching out, please review our Technical Support FAQ below to see if your question has been addressed by our preservationists.

Technical Support FAQ (Read First)

Q: I found a new SCPH model not listed. How do I help?
We are always looking for verified retail dumps from rare hardware revisions. Please include your MD5 hash and a high-res photo of the console's rear sticker in your email.

Q: My BIOS file is 8MB instead of 4MB. Is it fake?
Not necessarily. Some early Japanese developers units (DTL-T10000) featured an 8MB ROM for debugging features. However, for standard retail emulation, a 4MB file is the target standard.

How to Submit Verified Dumps

If you are a hardware collector and wish to contribute to the global archive, we follow a strict submission protocol to maintain the integrity of our database. All submissions must be accompanied by a log from a hardware-native dumper (such as DumpBIOS 1.0 for Free McBoot). We do not accept files that have been manually edited in hex editors, as these cannot be verified for architectural accuracy.

Our verification team uses a double-blind hashing system. Once you submit a file, it is compared against our shadow-archive of private dumps. If the CRC32, MD5, and SHA1 all align perfectly, the revision will be listed on our homepage in the next update cycle.

Reporting Malicious Content

The safety of the emulation community is our highest priority. If you encounter a link on a third-party site claiming to be "official" PS2 BIOS Hub content that contains malware or executable files (other than .bin or .rom), please report it to us immediately. Our BIOS files are never distributed as `.exe` or `.msi` installers. Real firmware is always a raw memory dump.
